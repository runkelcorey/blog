Thoughts of gym flood our nervous systems with dopamine, or adrenaline—or that other type of adrenaline that conveys horror from our brain’s primitive parts. Our differing responses come themselves leaden with heaps of remembered and diligently soldered reactions of prior gym times. A friend today pulled apart the crunches, deadlifts, and Russian twists we do for skeleton/muscle/fluid maintenance and those same diabolical words that we do for their own sake1—for our own sake. He argued there is a way to live that precedes (logically, though there are hints of a state of nature–type chronology as well, but for exercise) the gym culture

Mark Greif’s “Against Exercise” read as one of those essays the world itself cribs from, without citing.
